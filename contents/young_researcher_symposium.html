Young Researcher Symposium at EDBT 2026


The young researcher symposium at EDBT 2026 aims to replace the traditional PhD Workshop, shifting from the conventional paper-presentation format toward a more engaging, interactive, and community-driven experience. Recognizing that the value of a PhD workshop extends beyond presenting research, the new format is designed to foster active participation, peer-to-peer connections, and mentorship opportunities that support students’ long-term academic and professional growth.

Instead of focusing solely on research talks, the workshop will include a diverse set of activities that encourage meaningful engagement and knowledge exchange. These include invited talks by distinguished scholars offering insights into emerging research directions and professional development; a panel discussion featuring experts from academia and industry sharing practical advice on navigating career opportunities and perform job search in both academia and industry; structured networking sessions that provide opportunities for students to interact with peers, mentors, and senior researchers; and a personalized mentoring program that connects students with mentors based on their research interests and goals.

Through these activities, the workshop aims to cultivate a supportive and inclusive environment where PhD students can exchange ideas, receive constructive feedback, explore collaborations, and gain guidance on both research and career development. Ultimately, the redesigned EDBT 2026 PhD Workshop seeks to strengthen the sense of community within the data management field and empower the next generation of researchers to engage confidently and creatively with the challenges ahead.
